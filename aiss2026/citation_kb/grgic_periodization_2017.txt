VERIFICATION STATUS: CONFIRMED with CAVEAT
IMPORTANT CAVEAT: Our paper claims the meta-analysis found "structured load variation produces superior outcomes regardless of the specific pattern." The source actually found NO SIGNIFICANT DIFFERENCE between linear periodization (LP) and daily undulating periodization (DUP) -- Cohen's d = -0.02, p = 0.848. The finding is that the PATTERN of periodization does not matter (LP vs DUP produce equivalent results), NOT that periodization produces superior outcomes compared to non-periodized training. The source does NOT compare periodized vs. non-periodized training.

The claim "structured load variation produces superior outcomes regardless of the specific pattern" could be read two ways:
1. "Periodization is beneficial, and the specific pattern doesn't matter" -- the SECOND half is supported (LP ~ DUP), but the FIRST half (that periodization beats non-periodization) is NOT tested in this meta-analysis.
2. The abstract does state periodization is "meant to improve adherence to the training regimen, allow for constant progression" -- but this is a background statement, not a finding.

RECOMMENDATION: The claim should be more precisely stated as "the specific periodization pattern does not significantly affect outcomes" rather than "structured variation produces superior outcomes." The current phrasing implies a comparison against non-periodized training that this paper does not make.
