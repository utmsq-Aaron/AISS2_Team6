SOURCE CONFIRMS: Halson (2014) "Monitoring Training Load to Understand Fatigue in Athletes" (Sports Med, 44 Suppl 2:S139-S147) is a review article surveying fatigue biomarkers. The abstract states: "very few of these markers have strong scientific evidence supporting their use, and there is yet to be a single, definitive marker described in the literature." Section 3 reiterates: "very few of these markers have strong scientific evidence supporting their use, and there is yet to be a single, definitive marker of fatigue described in the literature." Table 1 lists 16 monitoring variables (frequency, time, intensity, sleep, psychology, biochemistry, etc.), making the case for combining multiple metrics. Both claims are well-supported: (1) no single unified interface/marker, and (2) multi-metric monitoring is necessary.
